    URLs are a crucial element of SEO (search engine optimisation) and by providing an excellent navigation experience the structure of the URL can help improvise the SEO of a website. Firstly let’s understand what is a URL? It stands for Uniform Resource Locator, they are used to identify the address of documents or other internet resources. These URLs are made up of multiple parts these being a protocol and a domain name which work to tell a browser about how and from where it can retrieve a resource (Waykar, 2020). An example of a URL is: https://sachin-govender-2262800.github.io/WSOA3018A_2262800/ and we can see the 4 components of a URL the Protocol, the Domain Name, the Subdirectories and the Individual Page Name this is seen as the URL structure it is the anatomy of how it looks, but what is the importance of URL structures to SEO is.

    URLs affect SEO, and is used by search engines to understand the content of a website. Googlebot uses URLs to rank, crawl and index, an optimized URL makes it easier for Googlebot to do just that. The URLs can also affect organic clock through rates (Larson, 2019). Now how can a website ensure that they follow the best practices when it comes to URL structures, there are 7 ways in which a website can ensure this. 1 a website can ensure they keep the URL length short and precise, ensure it is readable, Make good use of keywords, Avoid containing pointless duplication, use Hyphens in place of Underscores, Eliminate Excess words and lastly avoid Folder Mania (Vivian, 2019). There are a bunch of online tools that one can use in order to analyse a websites URLs.

    I myself have tried to follow these goals I don’t think my URLs are overtly complicated, the main information of the page is usually contained there, I make use of hyphens rather than Underscores, however I do think my URLs have quiet an excessive amount of words.
