    First let’s define what accessibility means, accessibility can be broken down as the ability to access something or the user’s ability to use something regardless of their condition. Web accessibility is the ability for all user’s to be able to access a website or app no matter their disability. (Emmanuel, 2018). According to The World Bank 15% of the world’s population or 1 billion people have some form of disability, whether it be hearing, motor functions, vision or cognition as well as the fact as many elderly people could also make use of having access to a more accessible internet, therefore it is vital that web accessibilities importance is not overlooked (Geley, n.d.). There are however 7 reasons why web accessibility should be implemented these are Legal, Reputation, Inclusivity, User Experience, SEO, Coding standards as well as the fact that it is just the right thing to do (Tim, 2020).

    In the legal case any digital platform that is connected to a physical location that provides good or services must also comply with accessibility standards this means that there can be serious legal repercussions such as with what happened with a 2020 Domino’s accessibility case. In this case a man named Robles was unable to order pizza from the Dominos app due to its lack of an alt-text feature (Andy, 2019). Having accessibility towards ones app can improve the reputation of a business a prime example again is Dominos which may forever be known as the company who had argued against the rights of a blind man (Tim, 2020). Inclusivity goes back towards the fact that the internet should be for everyone. The user experience as a whole is improved for everyone if a website or app takes a stronger stance in improving its accessibility. SEO which stands for search engine optimization, accessibility can be related back to google itself where search engine crawlers often look into the user experience that you provide and will often rank it according to this usability. With coding standards one understands that by having the implementation of accessibility from the get go it will create a stricter adherence to proper coding standards and thus the codes end result will be more performant and cleaner. The last point that it is just the right thing to do does not need to be explained with all the previous points above.

    There are many ways to make websites more accessible some examples include Alt-tags which is a description of an image, keyboard navigation for non-mouse users, closed captions for media which are subtitles for videos and audio on your site, having a simple design on your site that can be used by any user regardless of knowledge or experience. There are many more ways that a website can be improved to make it more accessible however if we went on with the list this blog could easily become an entire thesis based around the importance of accessibility.
